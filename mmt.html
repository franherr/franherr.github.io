Modular Multiplication tables

A modular multiplication table is a circular representation of modular multiplication by a positive integer a. To make a modular multiplication table, take a circle in a plane and place m equidistant points around its circumference and label them p = 0, 1,... m-1. Then chose a multiplier a and connect each point p to a*p mod m with a line segment. This creates a circle with line segments through the middle. We call this pattern MMT(m, a). This simple mechanism produces astoundingly beautiful and complex shapes.

There is a relationship between modular multiplication tables and a simple dynamical system. Imagine two planets A and B orbiting a centeral sun on the same orbit. Planet A orbits at integer speed α and planet B orbits at integer speed β. Then we draw a chord between A and B at every time t. We call this a planet dance. Then a modular multiplication is a discrete sampling of a planet dance. Looking at MMT's from this perspective reveal why many of their complicated visual patterns appear. This perspective is layed out in my paper on the topic.
